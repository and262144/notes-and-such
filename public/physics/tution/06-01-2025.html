Periodic Motion :- The motion which repeats itself after a fixed interval of time are called periodic motion.

Examples:-

  1. Motion of planet around the sun .

  2. Motion of electron around the nucleus.

3. Motion of Liquid kept in a U shaped tube.

  1. Motion of earth around the sun.

  2. Rotation of earth around it's axis.

6. Motion of the bob of a pendulum when it is displaced from it's stable equilibrium position.

7. Motion of a block attached to one end of a spring and other end of the spring is fixed and the block is slightly displaced from it's stable equilibrium.

Oscillatory Motion:- The to-and-fro motion or back and forth motion of an object about it's mean position between 2 extreme limits are called oscillatory motion or vibratory motion.

Eg:- 3. , 6. , 7. represent oscillatory motion.

NOTE- All Oscillatory motion are periodic motion but all periodic motions are not oscillatory.

NOTE- In oscillation frequency is small and i in vibration frequency will be more.

Periodic Function:- A function which repeats it's value after a fixed interval of time is called a periodic function.

Eg:- The sin and cosine functions are the periodic function only.

NOTE:- The tangent function though repeat their value after finite interval of time but at certain instances the values are non terminating , so tangent functions aren't considered periodic
